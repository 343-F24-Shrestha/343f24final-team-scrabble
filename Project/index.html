Scrabble Solver

About

This is a 'solver' for the popular, family-game-night board game Scrabble. This game originally made in 1948 by James Brunot was initially unsuccessful until the president of the Macy's department store at the time tried it on vacation. After that the game was ordered in huge sums. Brunot, now unable to keep up with the large demand sold the manufacuring rights to a larger company in 1952, which is how it evloved into the well known game played worldwide today.

This 'solver' is a may for players to figure out the best possible moves in a game of Scrabble at any time. This is a project made for an Application Development course (CS343) at James Madison University (JMU). In a group we worked together to form a helpful tool for all players.

Purpose

This 'solver' has many purposes. To be clear this was not created as a way for people to cheat in Scrabble. Though we do see the likeliness of this happening to be very high, the tool was originally made for other reasons:

  1. To check your score against the best best score to see what areas of your game need work.
  2. To practice against a computer in a way by placing tiles according to the best possible moves from a seperate tile set to yor own.
  3. The most likely reason, to beat that one friend who is really good at Words With Friends.

How to Use

This application is very simple to use. Follow all steps below in order:

  1. Go to the Solver page.
  2. Fill in the grid according to what tiles are currently on the game board.
  3. Fill in the seven Current Letters slots at the bottom of the page with the tiles that you have in your hand.
  4. Click on the Solve button.

That's all! Now you should have recieved the best possible word on the board filled in for you!
